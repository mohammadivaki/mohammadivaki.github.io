Curvature Equations

Time: Fridays: 10-12

Course Description

The Minkowski problem aims to reconstruct a convex body from its surface area measure. The solution to this problem is remarkable: a Borel measure \( \mu \) on the unit sphere is the surface area measure of a convex body if and only if \( \mu \) has its centroid at the origin and is not concentrated on a great subsphere. In this course, we focus on the smooth version of this problem and its close relatives. The aim is to provide the key components of the arguments for addressing the existence theory of an important class of curvature problems, the \( L_p \)-Minkowski and Christoffel-Minkowski problems.

Method of Assessment:

Oral examination
