Talks

Ricci Flow Applications

Speaker: J. Dott (University of Bonn)
Affiliation: Institute of Applied Mathematics

We consider the Ricci flow equation:

\[ \frac{\partial g}{\partial t} = -2\text{Ric}(g) \]

In this talk, we'll explore recent developments in geometric analysis with applications to manifold theory. Our main focus will be on the behavior of solutions in three dimensions, particularly examining:

\[ \int_M |\text{Rm}|^2 dV \leq C(M,g_0) \]

where \( C(M,g_0) \) represents the curvature bound depending on initial conditions.

Minimal Surfaces in \(\mathbb{R}^3\)

Speaker: E. Mäder-Baumdicker (ETH Zürich)
Affiliation: Department of Mathematics

Exploring the existence and regularity of minimal surfaces with boundary conditions:

\[ \Delta_g u + |A|^2 u = 0 \]

where \( A \) is the second fundamental form. We will discuss recent breakthroughs in the calculus of variations approach.
