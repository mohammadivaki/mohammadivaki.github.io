Curve-shortening flow

Time: Fridays: 10-12

Course Description

In this course, we study a beautiful geometric heat equation known as the curve shortening flow (CSF). Given a simple closed curve in the Euclidean plane. this time dependent process manipulates the curve by moving its points perpendicularly to the curve with a speed proportional to the curvature; convex points move inwards, and concave points move outwards. A celebrated theorem of Grayson states that CSF shrinks any simple closed curve to a "round" point. That is, the rescaled solution converges to a circle as we approach the maximal time of existence. In this course, a rigorous proof of this theorem is provided, and along the way, we learn about some important concepts in the theory of geometric flows, such as the maximum principle, singularities, Harnack inequality, as well as entropy and monotonicity estimates.
